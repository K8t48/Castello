ADA

Americans with Disabilities Act

The Americans with Disabilities Act was passed by the United States Congress in 1990. It was amended by the United States Congress in 2008. This is the law that protects the civil rights of people with disabilities in many aspects of public life including use of technology.

Does your company legally need to comply with ADA technology guidelines? All local, county, state and federal government agencies, any business that relies on general public for their benefit and privately run companies with fifteen or more employees are leagally required to comply. Failure to create an ADA-compliant website could open a business to lawsuits, financial liabilities and damage to your brand reputation. While the ADA does not offer direct guidelines for compliance the current industry goal is to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standard at the minimum.

WCAG Section 2.1 AA

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, also known as WCAG, are the standards for web accessibility and guidance on conformance. Created and maintained by the Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI) part of W3C, the group of developers who established the a substantial amount of the current mechanics of the internet.

Question of what activities online must be made accessible is unsettled and often debated, accessibility laws have focused on WCAG 2.1 AA as the standard for digital accessibility compliance. Created as an update from 2008's WCAG 2.0 the 2018 updated adds considerations for cognitive and learning disabilities, low-vision users, mobile device accessibility, and people with motor and visula imparments. It covers a wide range of recommendations for making web content more accessible. The success criteria are written as testable statements that are not technology-specific and are categorized into three levels of compliance: A (basic or minimal impact), AA (mid-level or “happy medium”), and AAA (advanced). At Castello Consult we recommend meeting WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance. WCAG 2.1 is the standard most large companies aim to meet. We stay up to date with the latest WCAG updates to provide the best accessibilty for our clients.

Section 508

Part of the The Rehabilitation Act of 1973

The Rehabilitation Act of 1973 was created to provide protection against discrimination on the basis of disability in programs conducted by federal agencies. The Rehabilitation Act of 1973 was amended in 1998 with the addition of Section 508. This new section covered all information technology, including computer hardware, software, and documentation. This amendment also introduced law enforcement measures to mandate 508 compliance for websites, documents, and applications developed with US Federal funding or for US government agencies.
